Small-cap stocks are getting renewed attention in this volatile stock market. After underperforming their large-cap peers for a decade, small-cap stocks are back in favor as investors rotate out of richly-valued technology stocks such as Nvidia (NVDA) and Microsoft (MSFT). So far in 2026, the Russell 2000 index (RUT) of small-cap stocks is up 3% versus a 0.50% decline in the benchmark S&P 500 index. Small-cap stocks are loosely defined as any security with a market capitalization of less than $10 billion. Stocks with market caps under $1 billion are known as micro-caps. Examples of well-known small-cap stocks include The Gap (GAP), Krispy Kreme (DNUT), and e.l.f. Beauty (ELF). Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) offer a cost-effective and safe way for investors to gain access to a wide variety of small-cap stocks. 
		Examples of leading small-cap ETFs to consider include the iShares Core S&P Small-Cap ETF (IJR), which tracks the S&P SmallCap 600 index and includes only profitable companies, avoiding risky speculation in the process. There’s also the Schwab U.S. Small-Cap ETF (SCHA) that tracks the Dow Jones U.S. Small-Cap Total Stock Market Index, offering broad, low-cost exposure to the sector.
