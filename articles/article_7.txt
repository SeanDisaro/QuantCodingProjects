Chip giant Nvidia (NVDA) is gearing up for its marquee annual developer conference, GTC 2026 in San Jose from March 16 to 19. Wall Street is buzzing with Buy ratings and optimism for new launches and CEO Jensen Huang’s keynote surprises.Key themes to watch include the full-scale Vera Rubin architecture with HBM4 (high bandwidth memory) and 3nm process for inference gains, potential Feynman GPU (graphics processing unit) previews, Agentic AI monetization and AI Factory updates, plus Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) and Language Processing Unit (LPU) rack reveals. These could solidify Nvidia’s moat, justify 2026-2027 sales outlooks amid competition, and fuel a stock rally, shifting focus from hardware specs to scalable AI ops.As the event approaches, analysts are pouring in their bullish views on NVDA stock with some even raising their estimates.
		Truist Financial analyst William Stein maintained his $283 price target, implying nearly 55% upside potential. He views the GPU Technology Conference as “a key positive catalyst” for Nvidia. Stein expects GTC updates on supply-demand dynamics, market growth, and trends in current and emerging semiconductor technologies. Moreover, he anticipates management signals that supply, production, and demand will align to fuel near- to medium-term growth.Tigress Financial analyst Ivan Feinseth raised his price target from $350 to $360, suggesting 97.1% upside. He believes that Nvidia’s leadership in AI data center infrastructure, powered by Blackwell and upcoming Rubin platforms, is driving powerful, durable growth in revenue, cash flow, profitability, and further gains in shareholder value, especially as hyperscalers ramp up AI Factory deployments ahead of GTC.Moreover, Bank of America Securities analyst Vivek Arya retained his $300 price target, implying 64.3% upside. He flags three GTC focuses: Feynman roadmap (2028); CPX/LPU solutions for inference/decode; and CPO optics in scale-up networks. Arya expects no 2027-28 outlook, but Rubin ramp signals could lift the stock post-Blackwell’s $0.5 trillion sales. Consensus eyes $0.75 trillion in data center revenue for 2026-27 and nearly $1 trillion for 2027-28.On TipRanks, NVDA stock has a Strong Buy consensus rating based on 38 Buys and one Hold rating.
