Ride-hailing platform Uber (UBER) is seen as one of the leaders in the move toward self-driving ride services, according to four-star Citi analyst Ronald Josey. He believes that the company has a strong chance of becoming the largest platform for autonomous trips globally by 2029. This is mainly because of Uber’s growing number of partnerships and its large global network. In fact, since reporting earnings in February, Uber has announced seven new or expanded AV deals, including a major partnership with Rivian (RIVN) for 10,000 robotaxis, with the option to scale up to 40,000 more.At the same time, improvements in AI are expected to make it faster and cheaper to roll out these vehicles. Notably, Josey highlighted Uber’s partnership with Nvidia (NVDA), which could help achieve fully driverless Level 4 systems. As a result, the supply of autonomous vehicles is expected to grow over the next one to three years, while Uber’s platform helps generate demand. In addition, Uber is investing in the infrastructure needed to support this shift, including a $100 million plan to build fast-charging stations in cities like Los Angeles, Dallas, and San Francisco.Looking ahead, both Uber and Citi believe that autonomous vehicles represent a huge opportunity. CEO Dara Khosrowshahi recently said that AVs could unlock a multi-trillion-dollar market, and Josey agrees that Uber is in one of the best positions to benefit. Even though the stock is down about 10% this year due to concerns about competition and pricing, Citi sees this as a potential opportunity for investors. Therefore, Josey maintained a Buy rating with a $110 price target and believes Uber can continue to gain market share as autonomous driving becomes more common.
