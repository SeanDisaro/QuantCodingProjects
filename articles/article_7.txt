Nvidia (NVDA) stock inched lower in after-hours trading Monday after it closed higher by about 3% during the regular session. The earlier gain came after Morgan Stanley once again called the chip giant its “Top Pick” and Nvidia said it will invest $2 billion each in Coherent (COHR) and Lumentum (LITE), two suppliers of optical components used in AI data centers.But sentiment shifted late in the day. Nvidia stock pulled back in after-hours trading after a Bloomberg report said U.S. officials are considering new limits on advanced AI chip exports to China. Following the news, Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) also slipped about 0.6%.According to the report, the administration has discussed capping Chinese companies at 75,000 of Nvidia’s H200 chips each. Shipments of AMD’s similar MI325 chips would also count toward that same customer limit.
		That figure is far below what major Chinese firms such as Alibaba (BABA) and ByteDance have reportedly told Nvidia they would like to purchase. These high-end chips are used to train and run artificial intelligence models, making them central to China’s AI expansion plans.While total shipments to China could still reach as many as one million units, most of that demand comes from just a few large technology companies. If each company faces a fixed cap, those major buyers would receive far fewer chips than they originally planned.In short, Nvidia rallied on fresh analyst support and strategic investments earlier in the day. But renewed uncertainty around China sales appears to be the reason shares slipped slightly after the closing bell.Overall, Wall Street has a Strong Buy consensus rating on Nvidia stock based on 38 Buy, one Hold, and one Sell recommendations.
