Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) is preparing for a major catalyst when the next Consumer Electronics Show (CES) starts on January 6, 2026. CEO Lisa Su is set to deliver a keynote address that will kick off the event and highlight the semiconductor company’s plans for the year.AMD is expected to reveal new product details during CES 2026, including updates on its processors and graphics cards. It will also likely highlight chips designed for use in artificial intelligence (AI) data centers. These components have been a huge boon to AMD stock in 2025 and could also help push the shares higher in the year to come.While AMD is set to lead CES 2026 with its keynote, investors will also be keeping an eye on Nvidia (NVDA). Nvidia’s dominant position in the AI graphics card sector makes it AMD’s main competitor. Any potential announcements made by Nvidia during the event could also have an effect on AMD shares. That means traders will have to keep track of more than just AMD’s presentation.
		AMD stock was up 0.04% on Tuesday, extending a 78.57% year-to-date rally. The stock has also increased 78.5% over the past 12 months.AMD stock trading activity today was below normal, as some 4.78 million shares changed hands. To put that in perspective, the company’s three-month daily average trading volume is about 33.12 million units.Turning to Wall Street, the analysts’ consensus rating for AMD is Strong Buy, based on 29 Buy and nine Hold ratings over the past three months.
