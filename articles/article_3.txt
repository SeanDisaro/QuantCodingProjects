Nvidia (NVDA) remains at the center of the AI trade and keeps making headlines. NVDA shares are up about 35% this year, with the stock rising 4% on Friday to $180.99 after U.S. regulators cleared Nvidia’s planned $5 billion investment in Intel (INTC). The move gives Nvidia more manufacturing flexibility at a time when demand for AI chips continues to run hot.Wall Street sentiment also remains firmly supportive. Top Bernstein analyst Stacy Rasgon reiterated a Buy rating on Friday, saying clearly, “We would be buyers here,” arguing the stock looks unusually cheap at current levels. Nvidia now carries a Strong Buy consensus, with analysts seeing roughly 44% upside from here as earnings forecasts keep moving higher. Given these tailwinds, it’s a good time to see who owns shares of NVDA.Now, according to TipRanks’ Ownership Tool, public companies and individual investors own 42.27% of Nvidia. They are followed by ETFs, mutual funds, other institutional investors, and insiders at 21.69%, 19.85%, 12.31%, and 3.88%, respectively.
