Tokenized stocks are heading to the States, but what does it mean for equity investors?Exchange giants Nasdaq and the New York Stock Exchange are actively working on developing tokenized options for investors and fintechs such as Robinhood (HOOD) already offer it to customers outside of the U.S.Another advantage is that it opens up fractional ownership of blue-chip stocks. So, you could buy a small slice of Nvidia (NVDA) just for a few dollars. This should further democratise equity investing, making it accessible to almost anyone from around the world interested in U.S. stock markets.
		Nasdaq has an agreement with crypto exchange Kraken. The goal of this collaboration is the expansion of stock trading into the blockchain. This would see Nasdaq offer tokenized versions of stocks with Kraken acting as a distributor for them.Nasdaq and Kraken have continued to work on their tokenized stock plan since it was introduced to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) in September 2025.
