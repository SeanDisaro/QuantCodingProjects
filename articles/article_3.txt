The chip market is getting more competitive as ARM Holdings (ARM) moves away from just designing chips to building its own hardware. With both ARM and Nvidia (NVDA) fighting for a lead in the world of artificial intelligence, investors are asking which stock is the better pick for 2026. While Nvidia remains the king of AI training, ARM’s new AGI CPU makes the company a major new challenger for data centers.ARM is a new face on the hardware side of the AI race. For years, the company stayed in the background by only licensing its designs to others. That changed when ARM announced it will sell its own AGI CPU directly to big tech firms.Guggenheim analyst John DiFucci recently raised his price target for ARM to $240, pointing to a potential 77.83% upside. He believes the move from being a passive designer to an active seller will help ARM make $15 billion in new yearly sales. This change allows ARM to compete directly for the huge budgets that companies like Meta (META) and OpenAI spend on AI systems.
		Even with new challengers, Nvidia is still the kingpin of the AI world. The company owns nearly 95% of the market for the chips used to train AI, which is the most difficult part of the process. Nvidia’s new Vera Rubin platform is expected to bring in $1 trillion in total sales through 2027.Nvidia’s power comes from its massive head start. Beyond just having fast chips, Nvidia has a software platform called CUDA that millions of developers already use. This makes it very hard for any new name, including ARM, to convince customers to switch their entire setup away from Nvidia.Choosing between ARM and Nvidia depends on what you want. If you want a new growth story, ARM is the entrant just starting its journey into making its own chips. Its focus on agentic AI, where AI acts for you, makes it an exciting choice for the rest of 2026.However, if you want the proven leader, Nvidia is the safe choice. It remains the kingpin because it controls both the hardware and the software that the entire AI industry is built on.
