A group of 149 retired federal and state judges has backed Anthropic, the AI private company behind Claude, in its legal dispute with the Department of Defense (DoD). In a brief filed on Tuesday, they argued that the government agency wrongly labeled the company a national security supply chain risk.The judges said the decision lacked proper legal grounding and failed to follow required procedures. They added that while the government can choose its contractors, it should not use that power to harm or punish a company, or to go beyond what is needed to manage contracts.The filing stated that the DoD misinterpreted the statute by designating Anthropic a supply chain risk. According to the judges, the label was not supported by evidence and had broader consequences for the AI company’s operations.
		They pointed out that Anthropic is not seeking to force a contract with the department. Rather, “Anthropic is asking only that it not be punished on its way out the door,” the brief noted. The judges declared that the DoD cannot use the law to “punish Anthropic,” warning that such labeling could affect the company’s dealings with other government agencies and private sector partners.  Joining them, employees of several big tech companies have also criticized the DoD’s designation of Anthropic as a supply chain risk earlier this March. Workers at IBM (IBM), Google (GOOGL), Salesforce (CRM), and other firms expressed concerns that the labeling could set a precedent for how federal agencies treat AI and tech companies.  Even staff at OpenAI, the company that took over the $200 million contract after Anthropic’s fallout with the Pentagon, voiced caution about the broader implications.  The recent case follows weeks of exchanges between Anthropic and the Department of Defense over the company’s role in government war systems. Following Anthropic’s supply chain risk designation, the AI firm sued the agency, arguing that the DoD’s actions were an unlawful retaliation. Before the lawsuits, U.S. President Donald Trump had directed agencies to stop using Anthropic’s AI technology, citing national security concerns. The order came after the DoD had already used Claude’s systems in U.S. military operations involving Iran.Anthropic is a private company and does not have a publicly traded stock until it IPOs.
