Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO) and Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI) are both core Vanguard ETFs, but they aren’t exactly the same. VOO tracks the S&P 500 and focuses only on large U.S. companies. VTI, on the other hand, invests in the entire U.S. stock market. Using the TipRanks’ ETF Comparison Tool, we have placed VOO and VTI against each other to find the best Vanguard ETF for investors in 2026.Let’s break it down.The Vanguard S&P 500 ETF is a go-to choice for investors seeking direct exposure to large U.S. companies. It tracks the S&P 500 Index (SPX), a benchmark that’s widely viewed as a snapshot of the overall U.S. stock market and the health of the broader economy.
		VOO’s top 5 holdings are Nvidia (NVDA), Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), Amazon (AMZN), and Alphabet (GOOGL). Overall, VOO holds 507 stocks with total assets worth $872.40 billion. In terms of holdings, VOO is more concentrated at the top than VTI. The ETF’s 10 largest holdings make up 38.35% of its total assets, compared to 33.65% for VTI. That means a bigger portion of VOO’s performance depends on just a handful of mega-cap stocks.According to TipRanks’ unique ETF analyst consensus, determined based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, VOO is a Moderate Buy. The Street’s average price target of $764.55 implies an upside of 21.1%. Currently, VOO’s top two holdings with the highest upside are Oracle (ORCL) at almost 90% and ServiceNow Inc. (NOW) at 75%.The Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF gives investors exposure to nearly the entire U.S. stock market. It owns thousands of companies — from large blue-chip names to mid- and small-cap stocks — making it a simple, diversified core holding for long-term investors.Both VOO and VTI are still top-heavy because large companies dominate today’s market, but VTI is slightly more diversified. In terms of volatility, VOO has a beta of 0.99, while VTI’s beta is slightly higher at 1.01. That means both ETFs move almost in line with the broader market. VTI is just a bit more sensitive to market swings, likely because it includes small- and mid-cap stocks, which tend to be more volatile than large-cap companies. Overall, the difference in risk between the two is minimal.
		VTI’s top holdings — NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, and GOOGL — are the same mega-cap names that lead VOO. However, the key difference is scale and diversification. VTI owns far more stocks overall, giving investors broader exposure beyond just the market’s biggest companies. Currently, VTI holds 3,467 stocks and manages about $586.33 billion in total assets.According to TipRanks’ unique ETF analyst consensus, determined based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, VTI is a Moderate Buy. The Street’s average price target of $410.90 implies an upside of 21.7%. VTI’s top two holdings with the highest upside are MetaVia (MTVA) at 3004.58% and PDS Biotechnology (PDSB) at over 2000%.Both VOO and VTI are strong, low-cost core ETFs, but the better choice depends on your outlook. If you want focused exposure to large, proven U.S. companies, VOO offers simplicity and steady performance tied to the S&P 500. If you prefer broader diversification and potential upside from mid- and small-cap stocks in 2026, VTI provides wider market coverage.
