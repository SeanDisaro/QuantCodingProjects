Chipmaker Nvidia (NVDA) has not yet sold its H200 AI chips to companies in China, according to U.S. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, as reported by Reuters. Although there has been strong interest, Lutnick explained that China’s government has not allowed its companies to move forward with purchases. Instead, the country is focusing on building up its own domestic chip industry. This situation is somewhat surprising given recent developments. Not long ago, China approved Nvidia’s H200 chip for sale, and U.S. President Donald Trump also allowed certain advanced AI chips to be exported to China, including the H200, in exchange for a 25% fee. In addition, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang had even mentioned that the company received orders from Chinese customers.However, shipments have still been delayed due to ongoing disagreements between the U.S. and China over the terms of these deals. On top of that, policy uncertainty is making things even more complicated. Lutnick said that the U.S. is still considering bringing back stricter export rules, including the “affiliates rule,” which was paused during trade talks. Therefore, companies are facing unclear rules around what can and cannot be shipped.
