Chipmaker Nvidia (NVDA) is once again the only publicly traded company with a $5 trillion market capitalization. Nvidia’s market capitalization again surpassed the $5 trillion mark on April 24 as its share price rose 5% to trade at $208.94 per share. NVDA stock headed into the market’s close with a market capitalization of $5.08 trillion, making it the world’s most valuable publicly traded company.Nvidia first hit a $5 trillion market capitalization on Oct. 29, 2025, becoming the only public company to reach that milestone. Remarkably, it has been less than a year since Nvidia first achieved a $4 trillion market cap. The microchip designer crossed the $4 trillion line on July 9, 2025. 
		It was only three years ago that Nvidia’s market capitalization first crossed the $1 trillion threshold. The exponential growth of the company’s market value comes as its shares have soared in recent years, fueled by insatiable demand for its microchips and processors that are used in artificial intelligence (AI) data centers. After trading sideways for much of this year, NVDA stock has caught fire again since the end of March. Over the last month, Nvidia’s share price has risen nearly 20% as investors move back into growth stocks, particularly mega-cap technology names. Nvidia remains the dominant maker of chips that power AI. Nvidia’s stock has a consensus Strong Buy rating among 42 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on 40 Buy, one Hold, and one Sell recommendations issued in the past three months.
