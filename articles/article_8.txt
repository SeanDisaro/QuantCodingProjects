Nvidia (NVDA) stock has often carried a “Perfect 10” Smart Score on TipRanks, reflecting strong analyst sentiment, technical momentum, and continued confidence in the company’s dominance of the artificial intelligence (AI) market. As long as spending on AI data centers and compute infrastructure remains strong, Nvidia’s leadership and the “Perfect 10” status surrounding the stock should remain firmly intact.The company’s recent numbers are a timely reminder of why it sits pretty at the top of the AI throne. NVDA is outperforming its megacap contemporaries so far this year, with the stock dominating the AI boom. As of late February, the shares were up 5%, while other megacap technology stocks have struggled as investors rotated into industrial and energy sectors. Furthermore, its frequent “Perfect 10” Smart Score on TipRanks underscores the stock’s high value.The chipmaker has consistently delivered exceptional results and remains the central force behind the global buildout of AI infrastructure. I am bullish on Nvidia stock and see it as well-positioned, but quite susceptible to a predominant theme: the longevity of AI infrastructure spending. Now, for investors, the crux of the matter is whether NVDA’s incredible earnings power can continue to meet or surpass the sky-high expectations placed on it.
		Nvidia’s latest numbers were outstanding, to say the least. The Q4 FY26 and full-year financial reports emphasized its importance to the AI cycle. “Enterprise adoption of agents is skyrocketing. Our customers are racing to invest in AI compute, the factories powering the AI industrial revolution, and their future growth,” said Jensen Huang, Founder and CEO of Nvidia.The company reported a record revenue of $68.1 billion, up 20% from Q3 and 73% year-over-year. The Fiscal 2026 revenue was $215.9 billion, up 65% from the previous year. The major revenue driver, the data center, which houses its AI chips, accounts for over 90% of Nvidia’s sales. Its sales reached $62.3 billion, up 22% from Q3 and 75% from a year ago, surpassing expectations of about $60.7 billion. Major platform shifts in accelerated computing and AI facilitated this increase. Furthermore, other segments were contributing as well. For gaming, Q4 revenue was $3.7 billion, rising 47% from a year ago. The Q4 revenue for professional visualization was $1.3 billion, up 74% from Q3 and 159% from a year ago, led by demand for Blackwell. Q4 automotive revenue grew 2% sequentially and 6% year-over-year to $604 million.Nvidia’s growth has come amid one of the most intense capex cycles the technology industry has seen. In fact, the combined capex of Alphabet (GOOGL), Amazon (AMZN), Meta (META), and Microsoft (MSFT) could approach $700 billion this year as the tech giants expand their AI infrastructure.Interestingly, Nvidia’s growth drivers span data center, professional visualization, robotics, gaming, and AI PC. Also, Nvidia has partnered with high-profile companies such as Amazon, Anthropic, CoreWeave (CRWV), Synopsys (SNPS), Groq, and Mercedes-Benz (MBGAF), among others, to continue driving innovation and AI adoption.While this sounds like excellent news, the risk is that this pattern may encourage investors to anticipate endless increments, which is impractical in any cyclical industry. Moreover, regulatory updates, such as tougher export restrictions on China, and geopolitical tensions already exemplify that not every demand may be met. Based on TipRanks analysis, NVDA carries a Strong Buy consensus rating, with 38 Buys, one Hold, and zero Sell recommendations from 39 analysts covering the stock. The “Perfect 10” Smart Score continues as of now.
		NVDA is a near-perfect embodiment of positive sentiment: Strong Buy consensus, double-digit percentage upside to the average price target, and an AI-driven narrative that keeps booming. But all of these could change if growth were to stall or if a significant competitor emerged with an enthralling price-performance alternative.Overall, Nvidia’s fundamentals have met and exceeded bullish expectations. Revenue rose in every quarter of Fiscal 2026, with data center sales increasing as hyperscalers and enterprises rushed to build AI infrastructure. Nvidia has turned its leadership in AI into revenue and margins at a level that few can aspire to. Nvidia’s performance and premium positioning give grounds for a solid case for its continued domination in the AI ecosystem.The most pivotal risk to Nvidia’s leadership status is the trajectory of AI spending over the next few years. If AI spending were to normalize sooner rather than later, the stock’s narrative could shift.
