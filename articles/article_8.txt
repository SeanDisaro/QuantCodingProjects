On Monday, top analysts at Morgan Stanley and UBS reiterated a Buy rating on Nvidia (NVDA) stock, expressing confidence in the semiconductor giant’s growth prospects. Morgan Stanley analyst Joseph Moore reiterated a Buy rating on NVDA stock with a price target of $260 (upside potential of about 43%), calling it a top pick, replacing Micron Technology (MU). Meanwhile, UBS analyst Timothy Arcuri reaffirmed a Buy rating on Nvidia stock with a price target of $245 (upside potential of 34%) following a meeting with the company’s CFO Colette Kress.Moore noted that despite continued strength in Nvidia’s business, the stock has not moved much over the last two quarters due to concerns about the sustainability of current growth and market share. However, the 5-star analyst contends that there are no indications that the ongoing hyperscaler investment cycle is over. In fact, Moore thinks there is enough evidence that the hyperscalers intend to continue to invest for “at least a couple more years.”“So we don’t buy into hundreds of gigawatts in 2029, but we also don’t see a peak in 2026, and our growth expectations for CY27 grow seemingly by the week,” said Moore.
		The analyst stated that Nvidia’s market share might fall this year, but demand for the company’s Rubin platform will remain strong, with shipments expected in the second half of the year. He expects management to address market share concerns at the upcoming GTC AI conference and demonstrate “a leadership roadmap that again puts Nvidia ahead of competition.”Based on his optimism, Moore moved from MU stock to NVDA as his top pick and believes the stock is trading at an attractive entry level of 18x 2027 estimated earnings.Following his meeting with Nvidia’s CFO, UBS’ Arcuri said that he came away bullish on the company’s growth in networking and its long-term margin trajectory. The 5-star analyst noted that Nvidia suggested that hyperscalers are already preparing for 2027 compute capacity expansion, with current demand trends already indicating another solid year.The chip giant also expressed confidence in the sustainability of hyperscaler capital spending. Arcuri added that Nvidia believes that the strong balance sheets and cash flows of leading hyperscalers enable them to invest ahead of revenue growth and AI operating profits.Arcuri highlighted that the company is very bullish on its networking ramp and already considers itself the largest global networking player, aiming to surpass the combined revenue of all other networking chip suppliers by the end of this year.
