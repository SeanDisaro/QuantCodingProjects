SpaceX, the private space exploration company led by Elon Musk, plans to continue large-scale orders of Nvidia (NVDA) AI chips. This commitment supports the company’s expanding AI initiatives following its acquisition of xAI. The announcement, made by Musk on March 18, 2026, highlights ongoing reliance on Nvidia hardware for compute-intensive tasks.Tesla (TSLA), also under Musk’s leadership, will maintain similar large-scale Nvidia chip purchases despite advancing its own in-house AI processors.Elon Musk confirmed on March 18, 2026, that SpaceX AI, the entity resulting from SpaceX’s February 2026 acquisition of xAI and Tesla, expects to keep ordering Nvidia AI chips at scale. This commitment comes even as Tesla develops its next-generation AI5 chip, optimized for edge computing in applications like autonomous vehicles and humanoid robots. While the announcement signals ongoing reliance on Nvidia’s hardware, it did not include details on specific order volumes or total spending. 
		Notably, Musk described Nvidia as a company he greatly admires, praising its rapidly growing valuation. Nvidia GPUs, including H100 and Blackwell series, have already powered xAI’s Grok model training and continue to support SpaceX’s AI workloads in space technology and data processing.SpaceX’s AI push intensified after acquiring xAI in an all-stock deal valued at approximately $1.25 trillion in combined equity, integrating AI models with satellite infrastructure such as Starlink for potential orbital data centers. Musk has emphasized space-based AI as a future low-cost compute solution amid terrestrial power constraints.This recent commitment to Nvidia sustains demand for the chipmaker’s products, boosting investor confidence in NVDA amid broader AI growth. For the private company SpaceX, it ensures continued progress in AI-driven innovations without immediate disruption from in-house alternatives.Although some investors are still hesitant to buy NVDA stock, it likely isn’t due to the company’s recent performance or sales. Nvidia remains the top global AI chip maker, holding a dominant market share of 70% to 95%.
