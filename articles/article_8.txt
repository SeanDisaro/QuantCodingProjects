Oracle’s (ORCL) stock is down heading into the market’s close on March 6 after a report surfaced that the technology giant is scrapping plans to expand a data center located in Texas.  Multiple media reports say that Oracle has ended plans to expand its data center site in Abilene, Texas as the company moves to cut costs after heavy spending on artificial intelligence (AI) initiatives over the past year. Oracle and privately held OpenAI have reportedly canceled plans to expand the flagship AI data center in Abilene after financing became an issue. Meta Platforms (META) has since stepped in to lease the Texas data center from developer Crusoe.
		Chipmaker Nvidia (NVDA) has apparently helped facilitate Meta’s discussions with the developer, paying a $150 million deposit to Crusoe and helping court Meta Platforms as a potential tenant for the expanded data center site. The 1,000-acre campus being developed by Crusoe in Abilene, Texas is part of the Stargate project, which was announced at the White House with U.S. President Donald Trump. While several parts of the site are up and running, Oracle and OpenAI elected not to go forward with plans to lease a large expansion.Oracle’s stock has a consensus Strong Buy rating among 31 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on 25 Buy and six Hold recommendations issued in the last three months. The average ORCL price target of $270.14 implies 74.76% upside from current levels.
