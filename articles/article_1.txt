Catch up on the top artificial intelligence news and commentary by Wall Street analysts on publicly traded companies in the space with this daily recap compiled by The Fly.STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP: Nvidia (NVDA) and Marvell (MRVL) announced a strategic partnership to connect Marvell to the Nvidia AI factory and AI-RAN ecosystem through Nvidia NVLink Fusion. The companies will also collaborate on silicon photonics technology. In addition, Nvidia has invested $2B in Marvell. The partnership builds on Nvidia NVLink Fusion, a rack-scale platform that enables customers to develop semi-custom AI infrastructure using the Nvidia NVLink ecosystem. Marvell will provide custom XPUs and NVLink Fusion-compatible scale-up networking, while Nvidia will provide the supporting technologies, including Vera CPU, ConnectX NICs, Bluefield DPUs, NVLink interconnect and Spectrum-X switches, and the rack-scale AI compute. For customers developing custom XPUs, NVLink Fusion enables a heterogeneous AI infrastructure fully compatible with Nvidia systems, allowing seamless integration with Nvidia GPU, LPU, networking and storage platforms while leveraging Nvidia’s technology stack global supply chain ecosystem. The companies will also partner on telecommunication network into AI infrastructure with Nvidia Aerial AI-RAN for 5G/6G, and advance world-class networking for AI, including advanced optical interconnect solutions and silicon photonics technology.After Marvell and Nvidia announced a strategic deal that will see the latter invest $2B in Marvell to collaborate on telecom and data center AI development, Oppenheimer analyst Rick Schafer said the partnership expands Nvidia’s AI ecosystem by integrating custom XPU paired with NVLink Fusion for high performance scale-up networking and views the deal as “a vote of confidence” in Marvell as a core AI partner in ASIC and connectivity. The firm, which also notes that Nvidia has been investing across the AI ecosystem recently with investments of $2B each in Synopsys (SNPS), Nebius (NBIS), Lumentum (LITE) and Coherent (COHR), reiterates Outperform ratings on Nvidia and Marvell.
