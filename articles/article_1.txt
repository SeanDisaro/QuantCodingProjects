The AI server space took a sharp turn last week. Super Micro Computer (SMCI) stock came under severe pressure after U.S. authorities charged a company co-founder and others with export control violations. At the same time, Dell Technologies (DELL) gained attention as a more stable alternative. As sentiment shifts, investors are now comparing which stock has the edge and where the better opportunity lies.Using TipRanks’ Stock Comparison Tool, we compare the two stocks to see how they stack up right now.The comparison shows that Dell appears more stable, while SMCI offers higher but riskier upside.
		SMCI, once seen as a leader in AI servers, fell about 33% on Friday and now faces rising uncertainty. The drop came after U.S. authorities charged a company co-founder and others with export control violations, which hurt investor confidence.The company remains strong on the hardware side and was an early mover in liquid-cooled systems. However, these legal concerns have weighed on sentiment.There are also concerns that Nvidia (NVDA) could shift GPU supply toward more compliant partners like Dell, which may impact SMCI’s growth. While demand for its products remains strong, near-term risks have increased, making the stock more speculative.On TipRanks, SMCI stock has a Hold consensus rating, based on three Buys, seven Holds, and three Sells. At the same time, the average SMCI price target of $38.89 implies about 89% upside from current trading levels.Dell stock gained 58% in 2025 and is up 26% year-to-date. The company is seen as a reliable AI infrastructure player, supported by its strong supply chain, deep enterprise ties, and long-standing partnership with Nvidia (NVDA). This gives Dell better visibility into future demand, backed by a growing backlog of AI server orders.
		Dell also benefits from a diversified business across servers, storage, PCs, and services. This helps reduce risk compared to pure AI hardware firms. With SMCI facing uncertainty, many investors are turning to Dell as a safer way to gain exposure to AI growth. In fact, Bloomberg Intelligence analyst Woo Jin Ho noted that “given the reputation damage, risks for share losses to Dell are heightened long term.”On TipRanks, DELL stock has a Strong Buy consensus rating based on 11 Buys, one Hold, and one Sell assigned in the last three months. The average Dell price target of $165.83 implies 5.18% upside potential from current levels.SMCI offers much higher upside of 89% based on analyst price targets, but that potential comes with elevated risk and weak sentiment. The stock still carries a Hold rating and a low Smart Score of ‘One,’ reflecting ongoing concerns around recent developments.In contrast, Dell has lower upside but much stronger fundamentals. DELL stock holds a Strong Buy rating and a high Smart Score of ‘Eight,’ showing solid analyst confidence and stability.For now, Dell appears to have the edge as a safer and more reliable AI play, while SMCI remains a high-risk, high-reward bet for investors willing to tolerate volatility.
