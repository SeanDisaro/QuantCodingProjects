The AI craze has created some clear winners, and few would argue that Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) isn’t in the pole position. The company has ridden a rising wave of data center spending to massive gains, and its share price has skyrocketed by more than 600% over the past three years.Nvidia’s GPUs have become the stuff of legend, with each successive generation creating excitement (and loads and loads of sales). The company’s most recent quarterly results pointed to continued growth, breaking a new quarterly revenue record with $68.1 billion in sales. This was up 20% sequentially, and 73% year-over-year.Indeed, much has been said about Nvidia, both the company and its share price. Rarely is the word cheap used to describe NVDA, however.
		That’s where 5-star investor Adria Cimino has an interesting observation, noting that NVDA is the second cheapest of all the Magnificent 7 stocks.Looking at the forward price-to-earnings ratio of these big-time tech stocks, Cimino points out that NVDA and META are at the bottom of the bunch, with multiples hovering around 22x.“This valuation may surprise you because Nvidia is the key player in the AI revolution and has been the stock that investors have flocked to quarter after quarter,” adds Cimino.While this appears a bit curious, Cimino has a few explanations for the relatively cheap price. For starters, there’s been an overall decline of AI stocks throughout the market, and Nvidia hasn’t been immune.Part of the reason was the inflated capex numbers among the hyperscalers, which sparked fears of a potential AI bubble. And though worries about the return on investment certainly plagued the sector, Cimino also notes that good old-fashioned profit-taking was likely one of the culprits.
		And yet, the investor argues that the pullback wasn’t linked to negative news regarding AI demand. In fact, quite the opposite.Indeed, numerous companies – chip designers, cloud services providers, and others – have “flocked” to Nvidia for products and services. “The AI boom continues,” exclaims Cimino, and that could translate into opportunities to buy some of the top names in AI at “very reasonable prices.”“And investors may want to start with Nvidia, the leading AI player that could become the biggest winner of the AI story over the long run,” concludes Cimino.
