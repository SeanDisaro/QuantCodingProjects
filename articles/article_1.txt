Catch up on the top artificial intelligence news and commentary by Wall Street analysts on publicly traded companies in the space with this daily recap compiled by The Fly.ALTERNATIVE TO GITHUB: OpenAI is developing an alternative to Microsoft’s (MSFT) GitHub, The Information’s Stephanie Palazzolo and Aaron Holmes report. According to the report, the project is nascent, and the decision came after OpenAI engineers experienced an increase in outages. SHOPPING RESEARCH FEATURE: Meta (META) is testing a shopping research feature for its AI chatbot that will allow requests for product suggestions, Natalie Lung of Bloomberg reports. The chatbot will respond to these queries with several product images with information on the brand, website, and price while also providing brief explanations on the recommendations. This tool will rival similar ones offered by OpenAI’s ChatGPT and Google’s (GOOGL) Gemini. 
		REVENUE RUN RATE: Anthropic is on track to generate annual revenue of almost $20B, more than doubling its run rate from late last year, Bloomberg’s Shirin Ghaffary reports. The company’s run-rate revenue has surpassed $19B, driven by strong adoption of its AI models and products, including Claude Code, according to people familiar with the matter, who add that the AI company is now valued at $380B. RELATIONSHIP AT ODDS: AI company Anthropic has served the U.S. government through its partnership with Palantir (PLTR) for over a year, but the Pentagon’s decision last week to restrict military contractors from using Anthropic’s technology could force Palantir to sever its ties with the company, The Information’s Aaron Holmes, Sri Muppidi, Rocket Drew and Julia Hornstein report. Roughly 42% of Palantir’s nearly $4.5B in revenue last year came from U.S. government contracts, the authors note. PENTAGON WORK: OpenAI CEO Sam Altman defended his decision to allow the Pentagon to use its tools for classified work, fielding questions from staff at an all-hands meeting Tuesday, reported The Wall Street Journal’s Berber Jin. Altman said that while he didn’t regret signing a deal with the Defense Department, he wished hadn’t announced the decision so quickly, telling staff that it looked “opportunistic” and “not united with the field,” according to The Journal, which notes that the remarks echoed a memo Altman shared with staff and on X Monday in which he said the deal “looked opportunistic and sloppy.” AI PLAYSTATION GAME ASSISTANT: Just months after Sony (SONY) applied for a patent for an AI PlayStation game assistant, it has been discovered that Microsoft (MSFT) has also filed for a patent covering an AI helper that can temporarily take over a gaming session on Xbox and a play a game for the user, Eurogamer’s Matt Wales reports. The Xbox maker filed the recently published patent application covering “state management for video game help sessions” back in 2024, with the patent filing also discussing the implementation of cloud-based help sessions that could involve text, chat, or video-based communication, Wales notes. MULTI-YEAR PARTNERSHIP: CoreWeave (CRWV) announced that it has entered into a multi-year strategic partnership with Perplexity to support its inference workloads on CoreWeave Cloud and pilot new services across both organizations. Under the agreement, Perplexity will power its inference workloads on CoreWeave’s platform. By utilizing dedicated Nvidia (NVDA) GB200 NVL72-powered clusters, CoreWeave ensures that its infrastructure keeps pace with Perplexity’s requirements of the Sonar and Search API ecosystem. CoreWeave will also roll out Perplexity Enterprise Max across its organization, enabling employees to search the web and internal knowledge, run deep multi-step research. and visualize and analyze data.
		NEW APPLIED AI ENGINEERING GROUP: Meta Platforms is creating a new applied AI engineering organization in its Reality Labs division to help bolster the company’s superintelligence efforts, according to an internal memo that was viewed by The Wall Street Journal, Meghan Bobrowsky reports. The new teams will be led by Maher Saba in the Reality Labs division, which houses the company’s metaverse and AI glasses efforts, and reports up to Meta Chief Technology Officer Andrew Bosworth. The teams are aiming for an ultra-flat structure of up to 50 employees to one manager, the memo said. AI-DRIVEN MANUFACTURING AUTOMATION: OpenAI’s former chief research officer is raising $70M for a new startup building an AI and software platform to automate manufacturing, according to people familiar with the matter, The Wall Street Journal’s Meghan Bobrowsky and Kate Clark report. Arda, the new startup co-founded by Bob McGrew, is raising at a valuation of $700M, according to people familiar with the matter. Founders Fund and Accel are co-leading the round, with participation from Khosla Ventures and XYZ Venture Capital. In a note after the report, Barclays noted the firm has received a number of investor questions regarding Arda. While software exposure to AI disruption has grabbed the initial attention, the firm thinks high-margin hardware is “the area that could / should be of greater potential concern to investors,” the analyst wrote. There are “two main direct exposures,” with the most well-rehearsed the direct threat to manufacturing software for the likes of Emerson (EMR) and Rockwell Automation (ROK) and the “more indirect threat, but arguably the threat that has the broadest application throughout MI,” being the AI threat to high-margin hardware, the analyst tells investors. Companies that have the highest exposure to high margin in-plant manufacturing equipment include Ametek (AME), Emerson, Gates Industrial (GTES), Ingersoll-Rand (IR), Illinois Tool Works (ITW), Parker-Hannifin (PH), Ralliant (RAL), Rockwell and Regal Rexnord (RRX), the analyst added.Published first on TheFly – the ultimate source for real-time, market-moving breaking financial news.
