Corporate insiders of American chip giants, Nvidia (NVDA) and Broadcom (AVGO), are selling millions of dollars worth of their company stock. The heightened insider activity has raised concerns among retail investors, especially amid ongoing volatility in AI-related stocks. Insiders appear to be capitalizing on the surge in both companies’ shares. Over the past five days, NVDA stock has gained 4.3%, while AVGO has risen 1.9%.According to Form 4 filings with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on December 22, Nvidia Director Mark Stevens sold 222,500 shares for a total of $40 million on December 19. At the same time, Broadcom CEO and President Hock Tan sold 130,000 shares for $42.38 million on December 18.A corporate insider’s share sale usually signals caution about the company’s future potential. However, an insider might sell shares for other reasons, such as financial or personal obligations. Hence, it is important to watch these trades for clues on the company’s growth expectations.
