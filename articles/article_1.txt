OpenAI is exploring a new step in its global growth plan. According to a Semafor report, the company may develop a tailored version of ChatGPT for the United Arab Emirates. This version would follow local speech rules, which could include limits on topics like LGBTQ+ content.In June of last year, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman appeared in Abu Dhabi alongside Nvidia (NVDA) CEO Jensen Huang. They announced “Stargate UAE,” a major project tied to a 1-gigawatt AI data center in the region. At the same time, OpenAI also committed to investing in the Stargate USA effort.OpenAI has said it wants to support what it calls “sovereign AI.” The idea is to help countries build AI systems that reflect their own language, culture, and legal norms.
		The company launched its OpenAI for Countries plan in May of last year. It said the goal is to “help interested governments build sovereign AI capability in coordination with the U.S. government,” and that it should be “rooted in democratic values, open markets, and trusted partnerships.”However, the UAE is a monarchy with strict social laws and a record of human rights concerns. That creates a clear tension between OpenAI’s global goals and the limits that may come with local rules.Meanwhile, the UAE-owned MGX investment firm is also an investor in OpenAI.
