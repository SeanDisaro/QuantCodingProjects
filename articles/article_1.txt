Technology exchange-traded funds (ETFs) attract investor attention as they provide diversification at low cost and offer exposure to companies in the tech sector with strong long-term growth potential. These ETFs comprise companies across growing areas such as artificial intelligence (AI), cloud computing, semiconductors, blockchain, and cybersecurity, among others.Here, we will look at three technology ETFs – Baron Technology ETF (BCTK), Fidelity Disruptive Technology ETF (FDTX), and JPMorgan U.S. Tech Leaders ETF (JTEK) that have a Strong Buy rating (based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on their holdings) and more than 25% upside potential.TipRanks’ ETF Comparison Tool gives a snapshot of these three ETFs.
		The BCTK ETF aims to deliver capital appreciation by providing exposure to rapidly growing companies that are involved in the development, advancement, and/or use of technology. It offers a unique opportunity to capitalize on the growth potential of both established tech giants and emerging disruptors.According to TipRanks’ unique ETF analyst consensus, determined based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, BCTK scores a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $32.93 for the BCTK ETF implies about 36.3% upside potential.Currently, the top three holdings of the BCTK ETF are Nvidia (NVDA), Broadcom (AVGO), and Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, or TSMC (TSM).The FDTX ETF seeks to offer long-term capital appreciation and invests at least 80% of its assets in stocks of companies focused on disruptive technologies. It is suitable for investors seeking exposure to a diversified portfolio of companies that are at the forefront of technological advancements, including AI, blockchain, biotechnology, and renewable energy. Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, FDTX has a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $50.05 for the FDTX ETF implies about 30.1% upside potential.
		The top three holdings of the FDTX ETF are Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (or TSMC), Nvidia, and Micron Technology (MU).The JTEK ETF seeks to deliver long-term capital appreciation by investing in technology and technology-enabled companies. The fund actively invests in innovative tech stocks across market capitalizations with underappreciated growth opportunities.Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, JTEK has a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $107.86 for the JTEK ETF implies 28.3% upside potential.Currently, the top three holdings of the JTEK ETF are Alphabet (GOOG), Lam Research (LRCX), and Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (or TSMC).Investors looking to compare performance, fees, and income across different ETFs can use TipRanks’ ETF Comparison Tool.
