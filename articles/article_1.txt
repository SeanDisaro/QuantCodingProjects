Micron (NASDAQ:MU) stock has taken a hit since its latest earnings release, dropping about 18% over the past five trading days and leaving investors trying to make sense of the disconnect. The pullback feels puzzling at first glance, especially given just how strong the company’s recent performance has been.Indeed, beneath the surface, the numbers were nothing short of remarkable. Micron delivered $23.9 billion in revenue last quarter, up 75% sequentially and a 196% year over year, while its $33.5 billion outlook for the current quarter came in $10 billion ahead of what analysts were expecting.Yet, Micron’s history looms large, with investors remembering just how quickly the cycle can turn, including the sharp drop from $30.8 billion in revenue in 2022 to $15.5 billion in 2023. The memory market has always been prone to boom-and-bust swings, and for many, that pattern is hard to ignore.
		On the other hand, there’s ample evidence that things are different this time around. The simple reason: AI.So, is Micron entering a sustained, Nvidia-like expansion phase, or is this surge setting the stage for another painful reset? One top investor, known by the pseudonym PropNotes, is leaning firmly toward the bullish side.“Micron’s profit supercycle mirrors Nvidia,” says the 5-star investor, who is among the top 1% of stock pros covered by TipRanks.The rise of high-bandwidth memory, or HBM, is fundamentally reshaping the landscape, according to PropNotes. With demand far outstripping supply – and only three companies globally able to produce these chips at scale, including Micron – the imbalance is driving what the investor describes as “explosive” growth in both revenue and margins.That mirrors Nvidia’s profit surge two years ago, when a similar dynamic occurred thanks to rabid demand for its wares from the hyperscalers.
		Moreover, PropNotes points out that every new generation of Nvidia chips requires more HBM per chip. That means the structural supply-demand imbalance won’t resolve itself anytime soon, further benefiting Micron.With Micron’s 2026 HMB supply fully committed under multi-year agreements, PropNotes isn’t too worried that the past boom-and-bust cycles will be repeating themselves.“The plague of ‘lumpy’ profitability could finally be in MU’s rearview,” adds PropNotes. Though the investor doesn’t necessarily see MU enjoying the same sort of multiple as Nvidia, PropNotes predicts that the stock will enjoy a re-rating of 16x earnings. That’s not due to the recent profit surge either, but a fundamental reset.“I see significant reason to continue holding the stock, even following the run-up we have seen,” emphasizes PropNotes, who rates MU a Strong Buy.
