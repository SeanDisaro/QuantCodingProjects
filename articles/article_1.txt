AI-driven demand continues to support semiconductor stocks, even as volatility and valuation concerns keep investors cautious. While Nvidia (NVDA) has long been the face of the AI boom, Micron (MU) is now emerging as a key player in the same ecosystem. Using TipRanks’ Stocks Comparison tool, we compare Micron Technology (MU) and Nvidia (NVDA) to see which stock offers better upside potential in 2026.While both companies carry Strong Buy ratings from analysts, Nvidia offers higher upside potential of almost 54%, compared with an upside of about 42% for Micron.Micron has gained nearly 452% over the past year and 32% year-to-date. The stock has seen strong momentum, driven by rising demand for high-bandwidth memory (HBM) used in AI systems.
		A major catalyst came in March 2026, when Micron began mass production of its HBM4 memory for Nvidia’s next-generation Vera Rubin platform. This new memory offers about 2.3x higher bandwidth while reducing power use by 20%, making it critical for advanced AI workloads. As a result, Micron is no longer seen as just a memory supplier but as a key partner in AI infrastructure.The company also issued strong guidance during its latest earnings call, projecting $33.5 billion in Q3 2026 revenue with very high margins. In addition, its HBM supply is already sold out for the year, reflecting strong demand.From a valuation standpoint, Micron looks attractive. The stock trades below 10x forward earnings, making it one of the cheapest AI-linked plays in the market. With 26 Buys and three Hold recommendations, Micron stock scores a Strong Buy consensus rating. The average MU stock price target of $536.40 indicates 42% upside potential.Nvidia has gained nearly 82% over the past year but is down 5% year-to-date, impacted by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and rising competition in the AI chip market.Nevertheless, Nvidia remains the dominant force in AI chips, with strong demand for its GPUs and continued innovation across its Blackwell and Vera Rubin platforms. The company is at the center of AI spending, with expectations for massive demand from cloud and data center customers. Nvidia’s ecosystem and leadership position continue to give it a strong competitive edge.
		However, the stock trades at a premium, with a forward P/E of around 37x. While this reflects its leadership, it also raises concerns about valuation. That said, analyst sentiment remains very strong.Wall Street maintains a Strong Buy rating on Nvidia based on 41 Buys and one Hold. The average NVDA stock price target of $273.57 indicates 54% upside potential.Micron stands out as a lower-cost AI play with strong growth drivers, especially in memory. Its improving role in AI infrastructure and attractive valuation make it appealing for investors looking for value.However, Nvidia still leads when it comes to overall upside and market dominance.
