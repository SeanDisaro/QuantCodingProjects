- H200 AI chips: China approves the purchase of Nvidia’s H200 chips for an initial batch worth about $10 billion, with buyers including Alibaba and ByteDance, signaling easing U.S.–China tensions and potential robust demand.

- CoreWeave investment: Nvidia is backing CoreWeave with a $2 billion investment, potentially lifting Nvidia’s stake to about 12–13% post-deal; CoreWeave aims for robust software monetization (SUNK and Mission Control) and over $100 million in ARR by the end of Q3, with a CRWV upside target around 14.7%.

- Waabi funding via NVentures: Nvidia’s venture arm NVentures participated in Waabi’s funding round alongside Uber, which is backing Waabi’s plan to deploy thousands of Waabi Driver robotaxis, including a milestone-based component from Uber.

- US-made, US-supply chain shift: Nvidia plans to move portions of its I/O die to Intel’s 14A process by 2028 as part of a Made-in-America strategy, with up to 25% of final packaging handled by Intel (EMIB) while TSMC handles the rest; Nvidia also invested $5B in Intel in 2025 to support this shift.

- Q4 FY2026 results event: Nvidia will report its fourth-quarter and fiscal-year 2026 results and host a conference call on Feb 25, 2026, with CFO Colette Kress providing written commentary ahead of the call; results and the call will be webcast and replayable.
