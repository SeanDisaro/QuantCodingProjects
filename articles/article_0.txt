Artificial-intelligence startup company Anthropic has reported that its revenue more than doubled to $9 billion between July and December of last year. The new revenue figure was disclosed as Anthropic raised $10 billion in its latest financing round. Management disclosed that the company’s revenue run rate has more than doubled since mid-2025, reaching $9 billion at the end of 2025, up from $4 billion in July.The latest funding round was oversubscribed, with investors committing more than the $10 billion target. When combined with $15 billion in previous commitments from technology giants Nvidia (NVDA) and Microsoft (MSFT), the total financing received by the company is expected to surpass $20 billion.
		One of the biggest investors in Anthropic is e-commerce giant Amazon (AMZN), which has committed $8 billion to the startup. As part of that investment, Anthropic uses Amazon Web Services (AWS) as its primary cloud provider and utilizes AWS Trainium and Inferentia chips to train and deploy its AI models.Leading the latest funding round were several venture capital firms that each invested about $1.5 billion in Anthropic. Along with rival OpenAI, privately held Anthropic is one of the leading AI startups in the world. There is speculation that Anthropic will hold its initial public offering (IPO) later this year, though the company hasn’t confirmed the timing of its market debut. AMZN stock has a consensus Strong Buy rating among 47 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on 46 Buy and one Hold recommendations assigned in the last three months.
