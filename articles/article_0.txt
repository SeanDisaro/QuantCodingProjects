Catch up on the top artificial intelligence news and commentary by Wall Street analysts on publicly traded companies in the space with this daily recap compiled by The Fly.CRUSOE LOAN: Chipmaker AMD (AMD) is pursuing the same growth-driving strategy that has boosted sales at rival Nvidia (NVDA), namely lending its financial support to upstart cloud providers buying its chips, The Information’s Miles Kruppa and Valida Pau report. AMD will effectively guarantee a $300M loan for data center and cloud startup Crusoe to purchase AMD’s AI chips and install them in a data center in Ohio. FRIENDLY TO AI: Meta (META) plans to spend $65M to boost state politicians who are friendly to the AI industry, marking the largest election investment by Meta, Theodore Schleifer and Matt Zdun of The New York Times report. The company was previously cautious about engaging in campaigns, but now with the company being a bigger threat in politics, Meta aims to beat back legislation in states that it fears could inhibit AI development. 
		FIRST PHASE: OpenAI is close to finalizing the first phase of a funding round expected to bring in more than $100B, Shirin Ghaffary, Josh Sisco, Edward Ludlow, and Natasha Mascarenhas of Bloomberg report, citing people familiar with the matter. As OpenAI prepares to spend trillions in infrastructure, the overall value of the company could exceed $850B, higher than the $830B initially expected, the sources added. The first part of the funding round will be funded by Amazon (AMZN), SoftBank (SFTBY), Nvidia (NVDA), and Microsoft (MSFT), with commitments expected to be near $100B if each of those companies invested near the highest ranges of what has been discussed, the sources said. LEARN AI WITH GOOGLE: Udemy (UDMY) announced the launch of its new Learn AI with Google (GOOGL) plan. This plan is said to bring Google’s newly launched AI Professional Certificate to more than 84M Udemy learners, “enabling them to master in-demand AI skills and learn directly from Google experts to meet the growing demand for AI skills in today’s workforce.” The company said, “Learn AI with Google is the first comprehensive AI subscription by Udemy for individual learners. This subscription offers access to an AI curriculum designed by experts at Google, coupled with a three month access to Google AI Pro for hands-on practice, all in a single integrated offering that goes beyond traditional courses. The all-in-one subscription delivers 3 Google AI learning pathways with access to the most advanced Google AI models. It includes: Google’s AI curriculum including Google AI Essentials, Accelerate Your Job Search with Al, and the latest Google AI Professional Certificate; Expert-led instruction from trusted Google AI experts with hands on activities; Access to Google’s most advanced models with three months of Google AI Pro at no cost; A chance to earn industry-recognized credentials from Google demonstrating AI fluency.”BULLISH ON TEMPUS AI: Mizuho analyst Bradley Bowers initiated coverage of Tempus AI (TEM) with an Outperform rating and $100 price target. Tempus is a leader player in precision oncology diagnostics and AI-enabled healthcare data services, the analyst tells investors in a research note. The firm views the company’s core market of precision oncology diagnostics as among the most attractive in healthcare with a $40B total addressable market that is growing at 30% annually.
