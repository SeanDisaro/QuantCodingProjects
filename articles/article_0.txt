Choosing between a mutual fund and an ETF can be tricky, even when both track the same index. Vanguard Total Stock Market Index Fund Admiral Shares (VTSAX) and Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI) both offer broad exposure to the U.S. stock market, but they differ in how you buy, trade, and manage them. For investors, VTSAX is ideal if you prefer a mutual fund with automatic investments and end-of-day pricing, while VTI is better if you want ETF flexibility, intraday trading, and lower buy-in costs.Overall, Vanguard is known for its low-cost and broadly diversified funds, offering several ETFs that analysts believe could benefit from favorable market trends in the year ahead. Investors can use the TipRanks Best Vanguard ETFs tool to explore popular options.VTSAX requires a minimum investment of $3,000 and trades only once per day at its net asset value (NAV), making it best suited for long-term investors who prefer mutual funds. In contrast, VTI has no minimum investment—you can buy as little as one share—and it trades throughout the day like a regular stock, offering more flexibility for investors who want intraday access.
		Investors who prefer the simplicity of a mutual fund and automatic contributions may find VTSAX a better fit. Meanwhile, VTI could be the right option for those who want ETF flexibility, the ability to trade during market hours, and a lower minimum investment. Let’s break down these funds in detail.VTSAX is a low-cost mutual fund that gives broad exposure to the U.S. stock market, including large-, mid-, and small-cap companies. It tracks the CRSP US Total Market Index, making it an easy way to own a diversified slice of the market in one fund. With an expense ratio of just 0.04%, it’s ideal for long-term growth, especially in retirement accounts or as a core portfolio holding.In terms of holdings, VTSAX holds 3,484 stocks, with total assets worth $2.06 trillion. Its top 5 holdings are Nvidia (NVDA), Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), Amazon (AMZN), and Alphabet (GOOGL). Additionally, VTSAX has a dividend yield of 1.12% and pays $0.459 as quarterly dividends.According to TipRanks’ ETF analyst consensus, which is based on a weighted average of analyst ratings across its holdings, VTSAX is rated a Moderate Buy. The Street’s average price target of $202.33 implies an upside of 24%. Among its holdings, the top two with the highest projected upside are BioAtla (BCAB) and NRX Pharmaceuticals (NRXP) at over 2,000%.
		VTI gives investors exposure to nearly the entire U.S. stock market. It holds thousands of companies—from large blue-chip names to mid- and small-cap stocks—making it a simple, diversified core holding for long-term investors. VTI has an expense ratio of 0.03%.VTI’s top holdings—NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, and GOOGL—are similar to VTSAX. The main difference is scale: VTSAX’s massive asset base offers slightly broader diversification, while VTI manages about $586.72 billion in assets.According to TipRanks’ ETF analyst consensus, which is based on a weighted average of analyst ratings across its holdings, VTI ETF is rated a Moderate Buy. The Street’s average price target of $411.34 implies an upside of 23%. Among VTI’s holdings, the top two with the highest projected upside are NRX Pharmaceuticals (NRXP) at 2,173% and PDS Biotechnology (PDSB) at over 2,000%.Both VTSAX and VTI offer broad exposure to the U.S. stock market.
