Catch up on the top artificial intelligence news and commentary by Wall Street analysts on publicly traded companies in the space with this daily recap compiled by The Fly:CAPITAL RAISE: OpenAI has held preliminary conversations with investors regarding a capital raise of as much as $100B, valuing the company at about $750B, The Information reports, citing people familiar with discussions. Valuation at that level would be 50% higher than OpenAI’s last share sale, the report states. AI CHIPS: Google (GOOGL) is developing a new initiative to make AI chips better at PyTorch, an open-source project supported by Meta Platforms (META), Krystal Hu, Kenrick Cai and Stephen Nellis of Reuters reports. The move is aimed to weakening Nvidia’s (NVDA) dominance in the AI computing market, people familiar with the matter said. This effort is part of Google’s plan to make its Tensor Processing Units an alternative to Nvidia’s GPUs. 
		ALL-IN BET: Sources detail CEO Mark Zuckerberg’s all-in bet to make Meta an AI leader, with some saying Alexandr Wang finds Zuckerberg’s micromanagement of AI work suffocating, The Financial Times’ Hannah Murphy and George Hammond report. AI SOLUTIONS: Cognizant (CTSH) announced a multi-year strategic partnership with Microsoft (MSFT) to help global enterprises become AI-powered frontier firms. Cognizant and Microsoft will co-build industry-grade AI solutions, jointly co-sell globally and collaborate on large-scale deals across key sectors including Financial Services, Healthcare and Life Sciences, Retail, and Manufacturing. The partnership will embed agentic AI and Copilot powered by Work IQ, Foundry IQ and Fabric IQ capabilities into mission-critical workflows. This partnership also expands on the Cognizant Neuro AI Suite of offerings Cognizant is bringing to the market which leverage Microsoft cloud and AI services. Cognizant will scale Microsoft 365 Copilot and GitHub Copilot adoption across delivery and consulting teams, while upskilling associates on Microsoft Azure, Azure AI Foundry, and related technologies. The collaboration will also harness Cognizant’s proprietary platforms, including TriZetto, Skygrade, and FlowSource, to deliver sector-specific advancements and modernize software engineering at scale.COLLABORATION: Dynatrace (DT)announced it has expanded its collaboration with Google Cloud to become a launch partner for Gemini CLI extensions and Gemini Enterprise. The Dynatrace Gemini CLI extension gives developers access to observability and root-cause analysis directly within their terminal.
