The Trump administration has concluded a semiconductor trade investigation citing harm from China’s industry dominance but opted to delay new tariffs, setting an initial zero percent rate on Chinese chip exports for at least 18 months, The New York Times’ Ana Swanson reports. The move reflects efforts to preserve a fragile trade truce with China following earlier tariff escalations and retaliatory measures.
