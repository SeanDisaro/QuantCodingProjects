Several investors are seeking exposure to technology exchange-traded funds (ETFs) to gain access to leading tech stocks that have attractive long-term growth potential. Technology ETFs comprise companies focused on rapidly growing areas such as artificial intelligence (AI), cloud computing, semiconductors, and cybersecurity, among others.Here, we will look at three technology ETFs – iShares U.S. Technology ETF (IYW), Vanguard Information Technology ETF (VGT), and Innovator Loup Frontier Tech ETF (LOUP) that have a “Strong Buy” rating (based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on their holdings) and more than 30% upside potential.TipRanks’ ETF Comparison Tool gives a snapshot of these three ETFs.
		The IYW ETF tracks the Russell 1000 Technology RIC 22.5/45 Capped Index and offers exposure to U.S. electronics, computer software and hardware, and information technology companies.According to TipRanks’ unique ETF analyst consensus, determined based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, IYW scores a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $254.90 for the IYW ETF implies about 33% upside potential (at the time of writing).Currently, the top three holdings of the IYW ETF are Nvidia (NVDA), Apple (AAPL), and Microsoft (MSFT).The VGT ETF tracks the MSCI U.S. Investable Market Information Technology 25/50 Index. It provides exposure to stocks of large, medium-sized, and small U.S. companies in the information technology sector.Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, VGT scores a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $995.24 for the VGT ETF implies about 36% upside potential.
		Currently, VGT’s top three holdings are Nvidia, Apple, and Microsoft.The LOUP ETF tracks the Deepwater Frontier Tech Index. It provides investors with exposure to companies that influence the future of technology, including, but not limited to, AI, fintech, robotics, autonomous and electric vehicles, and virtual/augmented reality.Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, LOUP scores a Strong Buy rating. The Street’s average price target of $99.96 for the LOUP ETF indicates 35% upside potential.Currently, LOUP’s top three holdings are Sterling Infrastructure (STRL), Siemens Energy (DE:ENR), and First Solar (FSLR).
