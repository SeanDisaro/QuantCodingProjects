Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) won’t sneak up on anyone’s radar. That’s what happens when you’re the largest publicly-traded company in the world. Yet, size and dominance do not guarantee a smooth trajectory. In recent months, the stock has faced periods of volatility, as concerns around a potential AI bubble, rising geopolitical tensions, and uncertainty over monetary policy have weighed on the broader tech sector.However, it’s hard to find fault with Nvidia’s performance. Not only are revenues still climbing ($68.13 billion last quarter, up nearly 20% sequentially) and margins holding at an impressive 75%, but the company continues to signal that more growth is still ahead.Just this month, at its spring event known as GTC, CEO Jensen Huang predicted that Nvidia would generate $1 trillion in sales from its Blackwell and Rubin chips by the end of next year.
		That’s more than $1 billion a day, exclaims one top investor, known by the pseudonym JR Research, who can’t believe how cheap NVDA has become.“If you have been awaiting a fantastic opportunity to load up, I think you shouldn’t miss this chance flashing in front of you right now,” posits the 5-star investor, who is among the top 3% of stock pros covered by TipRanks.The thesis, as JR frames it, goes beyond Nvidia’s dominance in GPUs. The projected $1 trillion opportunity does not even account for adjacent segments such as CPU-only systems, suggesting that the total addressable market could be even larger than what is currently being priced in.“In other words, Nvidia is telling us there’s a good chance that there’s upside bias to the $1T figure that Jensen Huang’s team has presented,” says JR.The market, however, seems to be overly worried that Nvidia will lose its pole position in the AI race. JR thinks it’s a mistake to be taken in by these “what-if scenarios,” arguing that the GTC showcased just how strong Nvidia’s grasp on the market is.
		After all, Nvidia has succeeded in integrating Groq technology into Rubin during the past three months, ample evidence to JR that Nvidia remains “the one to beat.” Moreover, its CUDA software lock continues to provide an impressive moat against would-be competitors.Unsurprisingly, JR assigns NVDA shares a Strong Buy rating “supported by NVDA’s rapid innovation speed, robust CUDA lock-in, and unmatched AI systems narrative.” (To watch JR Research’s track record, click here)Wall Street couldn’t agree more. With 41 Buys and just 1 Hold, NVDA soars to a Strong Buy consensus rating. Its 12-month average price target of $273.34 points to ~52% upside.
