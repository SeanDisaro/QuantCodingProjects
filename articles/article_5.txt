Micron Technology (MU) shares fell in after-hours trading yesterday and are down 6.5% in regular trading at the time of writing, despite beating fiscal Q2 sales and earnings expectations. The chipmaker is riding high on strong demand and limited supply for memory chips, with Q3 guidance topping consensus. Yet, shares fell as investors worried about plans to ramp up capital spending, though top analysts urge buying the dip.Mizuho Securities’ top analyst Vijay Rakesh raised his price target from $461.73 to $530, implying 14.8% upside potential. He cited Micron’s strong profitability and promising DRAM/NAND roadmaps.Similarly, TD Cowen’s Krish Sankar lifted his price target from $500 to $550, suggesting 19.1% upside. He urges focusing on Micron’s earnings per share (EPS) durability rather than fears that peak gross margins signal a stock peak.
		Micron crushed February-quarter estimates with $23.9 billion in revenue and $12.20 EPS, compared to consensus $19.7 billion and $9, respectively. Micron also boasts record 81% gross margins from soaring DRAM/NAND prices.Micron also guided for May quarter to $33.5 billion in sales and $19.15 EPS, which is 42% and 70% above consensus. Rakesh expects margins to hold strong into 2027 amid tight supply, with HBM4 ramping alongside Nvidia (NVDA). Micron’s new PCIe Gen6 SSDs are launching with 2x efficiency gains. Moreover, capex jumps to $25 billion for FY26 (up from $20 billion) and over $35 billion for FY27 to build new fabs, which benefits suppliers like Lam Research (LRCX).Micron’s surprise 5-year customer supply agreement (SCA) locks in demand, with more expected soon. Sankar dismisses “peak profits” fears dragging the stock. He noted that margins could hit 90% mid-2026 but stay above 50% past 2027.DRAM prices rose 65% this quarter and should climb another 35% next, doubling past highs, with more upside into late 2026 and peaks at 88-90%. The SCA’s length hints at demand through 2028 or beyond, even if not yet from a mega-cloud giant.At current levels, Sankar sees 20-25% upside from higher multiples plus $220 book value. He now forecasts $86 EPS for 2026 and $110 for 2027, up 22% and 33% from prior estimates.
