Bank of America (BAC) analysts have been updating views on several well-known stocks as markets look past 2025. Overall, the firm is focusing on companies with clear demand trends, steady earnings paths, and solid long-term plans. Here are three stocks Bank of America likes heading into 2026: OLLI, V, and NVDA. Ollie’s Bargain Outlet Holdings (OLLI) operates in the Consumer Defensive sector and continues to draw analyst support. Recently, Bank of America analyst Lorraine Hutchinson reaffirmed a Buy rating and set a $150 price target.The call follows a strong third quarter earnings report. In particular, profits came in above estimates due to better margins. As a result, management raised full-year guidance, which points to confidence in ongoing growth.
		At the same time, sales trends have improved. Comparable store sales rose as more shoppers visited stores and as the company adjusted its product mix. In addition, Ollie’s plans to open about 75 new stores, which support future revenue growth. The company is also using digital marketing to reach younger and higher-income shoppers.Bank of America’s five-star analyst Mihir Bhatia recently upgraded Visa (V) from Neutral to Buy. The firm set a $382 price target, citing better return potential after recent underperformance.According to the analyst, Visa remains a high-quality payments business. While new payment tools continue to emerge, stablecoins are viewed as a possible opportunity rather than a threat. In addition, legal and rule risks are seen as manageable. As a result, Bank of America views Visa as a durable business with long-term appeal.Meanwhile, Nvidia Corporation (NVDA) continues to hold a Buy rating from Bank of America’s top analyst Vivek Arya. The firm set a $275 price target based on Nvidia’s lead in advanced chips.Nvidia’s graphics chips are seen as well ahead of rivals. Looking forward, new Blackwell products are expected to drive stronger performance. Demand also remains strong due to partnerships with firms like Google (GOOGL), OpenAI (PC:OPAIQ), and Anthropic (PC:ANTPQ). Despite higher memory costs, margins are expected to stay solid.
