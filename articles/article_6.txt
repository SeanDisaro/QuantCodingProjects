The stock of Dell Technologies (DELL) is up 4% after the company announced that it is launching a new line of artificial-intelligence-enabled (AI) personal computers.Dell, which also makes AI servers for use in data centers, has introduced a new portfolio of AI computers that includes Dell Pro notebooks, Dell Pro Precision workstations, desktops, monitors and peripherals. The new Dell Pro notebook lineup includes models powered by Intel Core Ultra Series 3 and AMD (AMD) Ryzen AI 400 processors.The computers support on-device AI workloads and run Microsoft’s (MSFT) Copilot+ AI assistant.  The new lineup of computers comes as Dell and other companies look to further integrate and monetize AI technologies. It’s hoped that the addition of AI will boost sales in the market for personal computers.
		While Dell’s AI servers are experiencing strong demand, the company’s PC sales have slumped since the Covid-19 pandemic ended. Dell is betting that AI features will help to boost sales moving forward. Several of the new PCs also incorporate Nvidia’s (NVDA) microchips. Dell has introduced AI personal computers previously, with mixed results. However, Dell is betting that sales will pick-up as chatbots and other consumer-facing technologies improve. Pricing for the new computers was not disclosed by Dell. Most of the AI PCs will be available to buy between March and May of this year.Dell’s stock has a consensus Strong Buy among 13 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on 11 Buy, one Hold, and one Sell recommendations issued in the last three months. The average DELL price target of $168.92 implies 7.49% downside from current levels.
