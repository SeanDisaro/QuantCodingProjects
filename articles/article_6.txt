Nebius (NBIS) shares moved higher Tuesday morning after the company priced an upsized $4 billion convertible senior notes offering. This gives a clear signal that investor demand remains stronger than expected. The larger offering gives the AI infrastructure provider more financial flexibility at a time when competition and spending in the sector continue to accelerate.The offering was increased from last week’s $3.75 billion plan and is split into two tranches: $2 billion due in 2031 with an aggregate original principal amount of 1.25%, and $1.75 billion due in 2033 with an aggregate original principal amount of 2.625%.For Nebius, the additional capital is about scale. The company plans to use the funds to expand its data center footprint, invest in advanced compute infrastructure, and support the surge in demand for AI workloads.
		Importantly, investors see these moves as essential for keeping pace with hyperscalers and enterprise customers that are rapidly increasing their AI budgets.The timing also aligns with Nebius’ expanding partnerships. Meta (META) recently signed a multi-year AI infrastructure deal worth up to $27 billion, expanding an earlier $3 billion agreement. NVIDIA (NVDA) has also deepened its ties to the company, investing $2 billion for an 8.3% stake as Nebius continues to deploy NVIDIA GPUs across its data centers.By raising $4 billion, Nebius strengthens its balance sheet and boosts its ability to compete in high-performance computing, storage, and networking. With AI investment showing no signs of slowing, investors appear increasingly willing to back companies building the infrastructure that will power the next wave of AI applications.On TipRanks, NBIS has a Strong Buy consensus rating based on seven Buy and one Hold ratings.
