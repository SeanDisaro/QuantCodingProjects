Anthropic has accidentally leaked classified information about its artificial intelligence (AI) systems for the second time in one week. The new leak exposed parts of the source code backing its coding assistant, Claude Code. The recent incidents are raising concerns among users about how the company is managing its sensitive internal data. Anthropic said that some of the internal source code powering Claude Code was leaked due to a release-packaging error. The private company stated that the leak was not from a security breach and did not reveal any sensitive customer data. However, it could disclose to competitors and developers how the Claude Code tool was built. Developers use Claude code to write and debug software, making it one of Anthropic’s fastest-growing products. Therefore, any exposure of the core code is highly sensitive because it gives competitors a chance to figure out how the company’s most advanced tool was designed. The leak gained widespread attention right after the code links surfaced online, drawing millions of views within hours. 
		The recent exposure comes right after an earlier leak this week that unveiled details of Anthropic’s upcoming AI model, Mythos. The accident was caused by a misconfigured content management system. The previous leak also revealed secret documents and product information before they were scheduled for the public to see, highlighting Anthropic’s poorly managed internal operations. These recurring incidents are raising concerns among users about the company’s discipline, especially as competition with other big companies like OpenAI, Google (GOOGL), and xAI intensifies. To remain competitive and gain user trust in the sector, it’s important for any company to protect its sensitive data and development processes. Artificial intelligence remains one of the fastest-growing technology sectors due to the high demand for automation and enterprise solutions.
