Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) stock was on the rise Thursday as investors reacted positively to bullish analyst coverage for the semiconductor company. The biggest analyst news came from five-star Wells Fargo analyst Aaron Rakers, who reiterated a Buy rating and $345 price target, implying a possible 54.29% upside for AMD shares.Rakers believes that 2026 will continue to offer “insatiable data center compute demand” that will benefit semiconductor companies such as AMD. He also noted that AMD has a strong position in the CPU market and that its market share continues to grow. Additionally, Rakers pointed to the potential of AMD’s Helios server rack system and AI GPUs as more reasons for the bullish coverage. AMD also saw updated coverage from other firms today, such as Citi, which maintained a Hold rating and $260 price target, suggesting a possible 16.28% upside.
		AMD stock was up 2.53% in pre-market trading on Thursday, following a 1.19% boost yesterday. The shares have gained 4.41% year-to-date and 88.79% over the past 12 months.AMD trading activity today saw some 1.3 million shares change hands, compared to a three-month daily average of about 26.71 million units.In related news, AMD stock has also been boosted higher today on strong earnings from TSMC (TSM). This pushed several semiconductor stocks up, even as the White House tariff">announced 25% tariffs on advanced GPUs sold to China, such as AMD’s MI325X and Nvidia’s (NVDA) H200.Turning to Wall Street, the analysts’ consensus rating for AMD is Strong Buy, based on 26 Buy and eight Hold ratings over the past three months.
