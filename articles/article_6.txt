Shares of Nvidia (NVDA) are down at the time of writing, even though investment firm Raymond James became more optimistic on the chipmaker following its GTC keynote. While the short-term stock move suggests that some investors are cautious, the updated analyst view points to stronger long-term expectations. In fact, Raymond James maintained its Strong Buy rating and raised its price target.More specifically, five-star analyst Simon Leopold increased his price target from $291 to $323 after raising his estimates. This change is due to management’s projection that the firm will reach $1 trillion in cumulative GPU sales by 2027, which he believes could actually be conservative. At the same time, Leopold noted that Nvidia’s progress in AI inference is already supporting his investment thesis and may even be happening faster than expected.On top of that, when factoring in additional contributions from products like Vera Rubin Ultra and Groq LPX, the total AI data center revenue opportunity could climb even higher. In fact, Leopold estimates that it could reach around $1.3 trillion through 2027. Because of this revenue potential, he believes that Nvidia remains well-positioned to benefit from continued AI demand.
		Turning to Wall Street, analysts have a Strong Buy consensus rating on Nvidia stock based on 41 Buys, one Hold, and zero Sells assigned in the past three months, as indicated by the graphic below.
