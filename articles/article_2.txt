The stakes are high for Meta Platforms (META) heading into its financial results on April 29. Wall Street will be watching closely for any changes to the company’s capital expenditures on artificial intelligence (AI). Earlier this year, Meta Platforms said that it planned to spend $135 billion to buildout AI infrastructure, nearly double the $72 billion it spent in 2025.The eye-popping capex number is largely responsible for META stock’s tepid performance this year. So far in 2026, META stock has risen only 3%, trailing other mega-cap technology companies such as Nvidia (NVDA) and Amazon (AMZN). There are also questions about Meta’s AI strategy. 
		Analysts will no doubt expect Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg to answer questions about the company’s shifting AI strategy on the company’s earnings call. In recent months, Meta has made several changes to its AI plans as it look to gain a competitive edge with the technology.The company has acquired Moltbook, the social media platform for artificial intelligence (A.I.) agents; boosted its investment in a Texas AI data center by more than sixfold to $10 billion; launched a new AI model called Muse Spark; made several changes to its Superintelligence Labs; and announced multiple rounds of employee layoffs. The stock of Meta Platforms has a consensus Strong Buy rating among 45 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on 39 Buy and six Hold recommendations issued in the last three months.
