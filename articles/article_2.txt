Databricks, a leading data and AI company, has acquired two cybersecurity startups, Antimatter and SiftD.ai, to underpin the launch of its new AI security platform, Lakewatch. The move comes shortly after the company secured a $5 billion funding round, strengthening its position to invest in new capabilities and scale its platform.In a March 24 announcement, Databricks, best known for its cloud data analytics platform, unveiled Lakewatch, a new AI security platform that represents the company’s strategic entry into cybersecurity. The platform combines Databricks’ large-scale data storage capabilities with traditional Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) functions, such as threat detection and investigation. Powered by AI agents running on Anthropic’s Claude AI models, the system enables more advanced, automated security analysis. A Databricks spokesperson stated that Lakewatch positions Databricks at “the nexus of data platforms, AI agents, and cybersecurity, meeting enterprise demands for tools that can match the speed and sophistication of modern threats.”
		To build Lakewatch, Databricks integrated technologies and talent from two strategic acquisitions: Antimatter and SiftD.ai. The company acquired Antimatter in an undisclosed deal that closed last year but was only disclosed now, while the SiftD.ai acquisition came together rapidly over recent weeks, closing on March 23.According to sources familiar with the matter, both acquisitions were designed to bring critical cybersecurity expertise into Databricks’ expanding security division. Antimatter was developing a sophisticated “data control plane” technology that enables the safe deployment of AI agents around sensitive data through advanced policies, including redaction, tokenization, and context boundaries. The deal likely included intellectual property as well. Founded by UC Berkeley security researcher Andrew Krioukov, Antimatter had raised $12 million in 2022 in a round led by New Enterprise Associates. Meanwhile, SiftD.ai brought deep expertise from veterans from Splunk, a subsidiary of Cisco (CSCO), who created the company’s Search Processing Language and built its search infrastructure for large-scale threat detection. The startup was notably young, having launched its collaborative notebook product only in November 2025. While Databricks declined to specify exactly how many employees it acquired from the deals, the AI company confirmed that key personnel from both startups joined the organization to strengthen its security capabilities for Lakewatch. Krioukov now leads the Lakewatch development team, bringing his cybersecurity research background directly into Databricks’ product development.While Databricks is still a private company ahead of a potential initial public offering (IPO) in 2026, investors interested in AI stocks can consider top players such as Nvidia (NVDA), Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM), and Amazon (AMZN), all rated Strong Buys by Wall Street analysts tracked on TipRanks Stocks Comparison Center.
