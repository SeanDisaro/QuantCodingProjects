Catch up on the top artificial intelligence news and commentary by Wall Street analysts on publicly traded companies in the space with this daily recap compiled by The Fly.ANTHROPIC SUES PENTAGON: AI company Anthropic has filed two legal complaints against the U.S. Department of Defense in California and Washington, D.C. following a weeks-long conflict between the two parties over whether the military should have unrestricted access to Anthropic’s AI systems, TechCrunch’s Rebecca Bellan reports. The conflict arose after the Claude maker stated that it didn’t want its technology to be used for mass surveillance of Americans and didn’t believe it was prepared to power fully autonomous weapons with no humans making targeting and firing decisions. In response, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said that the Pentagon should have access to Anthropic’s AI systems for “any lawful purpose,” with the agency labeling Anthropic a supply chain risk last week, the author notes. ACQUISITION: OpenAI announced, “We’re acquiring Promptfoo, an AI security platform that helps enterprises identify and remediate vulnerabilities in AI systems during development. Once the acquisition is finalized, we will integrate Promptfoo’s technology directly into OpenAI Frontier, our platform for building and operating AI coworkers… The Promptfoo team, led by Ian Webster and Michael D’Angelo, has built a powerful suite of tools trusted by over 25 percent of Fortune 500 companies, along with a widely used open-source CLI and library for evaluating and red-teaming LLM applications. Together, we will continue building the open-source project while also advancing the integrated enterprise capabilities within Frontier.” 
		RESIGNATION: The head of OpenAI’s robotics team, Caitlin Kalinowski, confirmed her resignation, saying in a post on X, formerly Twitter, “I resigned from OpenAI. I care deeply about the Robotics team and the work we built together. This wasn’t an easy call. AI has an important role in national security. But surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and lethal autonomy without human authorization are lines that deserved more deliberation than they got. This was about principle, not people. I have deep respect for Sam and the team, and I’m proud of what we built together.” DATA CENTER IN TEXAS: Oracle (ORCL) and OpenAI have scrapped plans to expand an artificial intelligence data center in Texas after negotiations dragged over financing and OpenAI’s changing needs, sources told Bloomberg Brody Ford, Ed Ludlow and Dina Bass. This created an opening for Meta Platforms (META) to step in and consider leasing the planned expansion site from developer Crusoe after Nvidia (NVDA) helped facilitate Meta’s discussions with the developer, the news service reported on Friday afternoon.FUNDING ROUND: Together AI, a major cloud provider renting Nvidia (NVDA) chip servers to AI developers, is in advanced talks to raise roughly $1B in new funding at a pre-money valuation of about $7.5B, The Information’s Valida Pau, Stephanie Palazzolo and Anissa Gardizy report. The new funding round would more than double the startup’s valuation from a year ago. AI DEALS: Samsung’s (SSNLF) consumer device chief TM Roh says the company is “open to strategic co-operation” with more AI groups, having recently added Perplexity to its mobile OS, in an effort to challenge Apple’s (AAPL) smartphone lead, The Financial Times’ Michael Acton reports. Published first on TheFly – the ultimate source for real-time, market-moving breaking financial news.
