Nvidia (NVDA) CEO Jensen Huang said that the chipmaker’s recent $30 billion investment in OpenAI may be its final investment in the artificial intelligence startup before it potentially goes public later this year. Speaking at the Morgan Stanley (MS) Technology, Media & Telecom Conference, Huang explained that Nvidia had previously discussed the possibility of investing as much as $100 billion in OpenAI as part of an infrastructure partnership. However, he noted that such a large investment is now unlikely, mostly because OpenAI is expected to pursue an IPO in the near future.At the same time, Huang indicated that Nvidia may also be done making large investments in other AI startups. In particular, he suggested that the company’s $10 billion investment in Anthropic, which is another major OpenAI competitor, will likely be its final funding commitment to that company as well. It is also worth mentioning that Nvidia’s $30 billion investment in OpenAI was part of a massive $110 billion funding round. The round included major commitments from other tech giants, including $50 billion from Amazon (AMZN) and $30 billion from SoftBank (SFTBF).However, there have been questions about Nvidia’s long-term partnership with OpenAI for several months now. In fact, the speculation began back in November, when Nvidia disclosed in a quarterly filing that the previously discussed $100 billion investment deal might not actually happen. The company also disclosed in its February filing that there is no guarantee that an investment or partnership agreement with OpenAI will ultimately be completed. As a result, while Nvidia remains closely tied to the AI ecosystem, its future involvement with OpenAI is still uncertain.
