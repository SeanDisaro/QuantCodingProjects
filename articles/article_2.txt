Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC) (TSM) saw its January sales soar 37% to NT$401.3 billion ($12.7 billion), driven by strong global AI spending. The jump exceeded TSMC’s earlier forecast of about 30% revenue growth for the full year. Following the announcement, TSMC’s U.S.-listed stock gained nearly 3% as of this writing. For context, TSMC is the world’s largest contract chipmaker, supplying advanced semiconductors to top tech giants, including Apple (AAPL), Nvidia (NVDA), and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD). The company has been a major winner from the boom in AI spending, thanks to its role in building advanced AI accelerators.Looking ahead, TSMC plans to spend up to $56 billion on capital expenditures this year, a 30% increase from 2025. About 70% to 80% of this spending will go toward advanced technologies, highlighting its focus on next-generation chip manufacturing. In addition, the company expects high spending over the next three years to meet growing demand for AI chips.
		Additionally, TSMC could also benefit from U.S. policy. The company may be exempt from upcoming semiconductor tariffs thanks to its $165 billion U.S. investment pledge.In 2025, TSMC continued to focus on its most advanced chips. Products made with 7-nanometer processes or smaller accounted for 74% of wafer revenue for the full year, up from 69% in 2024, showing the company’s shift toward cutting-edge semiconductor technology.Meanwhile, High-performance computing (HPC) platforms—including AI data center accelerators—drove most of the growth. HPC revenue rose 48% year-over-year and made up 58% of total revenue in 2025.This focus on high-end chips highlights the growing importance of AI applications for TSMC’s overall revenue growth.According to TipRanks, TSM stock has a Strong Buy consensus rating based on six Buys and one Hold assigned in the last three months. At $267.6, the TSMC average stock price target implies a 10% upside potential.
