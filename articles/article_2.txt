Investors seeking to capitalize on secular growth prospects in artificial intelligence (AI), cloud computing, quantum computing, semiconductors, cybersecurity, and several other emerging areas can consider adding technology exchange-traded funds (ETFs) to their portfolios. These ETFs provide the benefits of diversification and lower costs.  Here, we will look at three technology ETFs – Global X Artificial Intelligence & Technology ETF (AIQ), Horizon Digital Frontier ETF (YNOT), and iShares U.S. Tech Breakthrough Multisector ETF (TECB).TipRanks’ ETF Comparison Tool gives a snapshot of these three ETFs.
		The AIQ ETF tracks the Indxx Artificial Intelligence & Big Data Index and offers exposure to companies expected to benefit from the further development and use of AI in their products and services, and to companies that provide hardware that supports the use of AI for the analysis of big data.According to TipRanks’ unique ETF analyst consensus, determined based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, AIQ scores a Moderate Buy rating. The average price target of $64.38 for the AIQ ETF implies about 29% upside potential.Currently, the top three holdings of the AIQ ETF are Samsung Electronics (SSNLF), SK Hynix, and Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSM).The YNOT ETF offers exposure to stocks of U.S. and foreign companies focused on transformative digital technologies, including AI, blockchain, quantum computing, and cybersecurity.Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, YNOT has a Strong Buy rating. The average price target of $35.40 for the YNOT ETF implies 23.7% upside potential.
		Currently, the top three holdings of the YNOT ETF are Alphabet (GOOG), Nvidia (NVDA), and Amazon (AMZN).The TECB ETF tracks the NYSE FactSet U.S. Tech Breakthrough Index (USD) and offers exposure to U.S. companies that could benefit from various breakthrough technologies, including robotics and AI, cloud and data tech, cybersecurity, genomics and immunology, and financial technology.Based on a weighted average of analyst ratings on its holdings, TECB has a Moderate Buy rating.
