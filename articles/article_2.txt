Wells Fargo analyst Aaron Rakers has raised his price target on Nvidia (NVDA) stock to $265 (39.3% upside) from $220, while maintaining a Buy rating ahead of the chipmaker’s fiscal Q3 earnings report. Needless to say, the five-star analyst is optimistic about Nvidia’s long-term growth, particularly in the AI-driven data center space.In a note to investors, Rakers has made only slight adjustments to near-term estimates, but significantly raised projections for Fiscal 2027 and Fiscal 2028. The move comes as major cloud providers ramp up investments in AI infrastructure.Wells Fargo now models:
		The key reason for this optimism is Nvidia’s ability to scale up supply and maintain strong visibility into demand through 2026 and beyond. Further, the analyst suggests Nvidia’s 2027 EPS could climb above $9, slightly higher than the analyst’s current forecast of $8.90.The updated $265 price target is based on a 30x P/E multiple on CY27 earnings, reflecting Nvidia’s “best-in-class positioning” in the ongoing AI and data center investment cycle.On TipRanks, NVDA stock has received a Strong Buy consensus rating, with 37 Buys, one Hold, and one Sell assigned in the last three months.
