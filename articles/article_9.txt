At a conference on Wednesday, Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) CEO Jensen Huang said demand for the company’s products was “higher than very high.”Such a statement might sound hyperbolic, but that claim probably won’t come as much of a surprise to Tigress Financial’s Ivan Feinseth, who has just increased his price target for NVDA from $350 to a new Street-high of $360, suggesting the shares will rise by 97% over the coming months. Naturally, Feinseth’s rating stays a Buy. (To watch Feinseth’s track record, click here)Simply put, Nvidia is at the forefront of GPU accelerators and full systems for AI training and inference, serving hyperscalers, enterprises, and sovereign AI projects. Its Data Center business has become the major revenue driver and is still “growing rapidly.” As Blackwell “ramps into volume” for these same customers, and future architectures like Rubin/Vera Rubin and Feynman reduce inference costs and increase throughput, Feinseth says Nvidia “sustains an ongoing AI capital investment cycle that widens its moat and supports premium pricing.”
		At the same time, the company’s ecosystem – including CUDA, cuDNN, and a wide range of AI frameworks and SDKs – supports millions of developers and thousands of enterprise applications, creating high switching costs and strong customer loyalty. On the enterprise side, offerings like Nvidia AI Enterprise and vertical stacks for healthcare, industrial digital twins, and robotics generate recurring software and service revenue alongside hardware sales.Feinseth also points out that platforms like Omniverse and industrial digital twins further fuel GPU and AI demand in design, simulation, and factory optimization, linking real-world applications back to the data center. Meanwhile, Nvidia’s DRIVE and robotics platforms bring AI to the edge, and sovereign AI projects expand its reach into automotive, logistics, and national compute infrastructure, broadening the company’s total addressable market.There’s so much money being spent on AI-related endeavors, and much of it is heading Nvidia’s way. Aggregate capital investment from leading hyperscalers and cloud providers is expected to exceed $650 billion in 2026, with Nvidia capturing a significant portion through its industry-leading GPUs. The total AI market is expected to reach $3–$4 trillion by 2030 as hyperscalers, enterprises, and sovereigns reconfigure data centers into AI “factories.”Moreover, high-value networking and system attachments – like NVLink, NVL72, Spectrum‑X, and InfiniBand – along with hardware-software co-design and the CUDA/AI Enterprise ecosystem, create “deep platform lock-in.”“Combined with exceptional free‑cash‑flow generation, disciplined reinvestment in R&D and capacity, and robust capital returns through buybacks and dividends, these growth engines drive rising returns on capital and expanding economic Profit, translating into accelerating long‑term shareholder value creation,” the 5-star analyst summed up.
		So, that’s the Tigress view and it is one shared by most other analysts. All told, the stock claims a Strong Buy consensus rating, based on a mix of 39 Buys vs. 1 Hold and Sell, each. The forecast calls for 12-month returns of 48%, considering the average target clocks in at $272.16.
